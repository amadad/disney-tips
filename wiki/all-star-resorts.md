# All-Star Resorts

Disney World's cheapest hotel tier.
Three separate **value resorts** grouped in the same zone:
**All-Star Movies**, **All-Star Music**, and **All-Star Sports**.

## What they are for

The All-Stars are for guests who want:

- the **lowest Disney price point**
- simple rooms
- late-night food-court access
- a hotel that is mostly a launch pad for the parks

## Shared tradeoffs

All three rely on **buses only** for Disney transportation.
During some periods, one bus may loop across all three resorts before
heading to the parks, which creates a real difference between them.

Dining is also simplified here:

- one main food court per resort
- broad, crowd-pleasing menus
- later hours than many resort dining spots, often until **11 PM**

## Extra value-resort operational notes

DFBGuide 2026-03 adds two useful operational details:

- eligible **domestic Southwest flyers** using Orlando International can
  use Disney's value-resort baggage-skip flow instead of regular baggage
  claim friction
- **All-Star Sports** had the Surfboard Bay pool and kiddie pool closed
  through **April 2026**, with the Grand Slam leisure pool still open

Because the All-Stars function as a special cluster, guests can also
**pool hop among the three All-Star resorts** during these closure
periods — not a normal Disney-wide rule.

## The differences that matter

### All-Star Music

DFBGuide's favorite of the trio because it is the only one with the
cheaper **family suite** lane.

Family suites here:

- start around **$400/night** per the source
- sleep up to **6 guests**
- include two pull-down living-room beds, a separate bedroom, two
  bathrooms, and a kitchenette with a full-size fridge

This is the strongest All-Star option for larger families who do not
want Art of Animation prices.

### All-Star Movies

Best-known standout from this source: the **smart locker delivery
system**, which is especially useful for families ordering groceries or
package delivery.

### All-Star Sports

DFBGuide's practical edge case: this may be the best of the three for
**bus convenience**, because Sports is often the first stop in the
shared-bus loop. That can mean first shot at seats in the morning and
first drop-off at night.

## Best for

- budget-first trips
- large families using All-Star Music suites
- guests who want Disney theming without paying Skyliner or deluxe rates

## Not best for

- guests who hate buses
- travelers who want strong dining or resort-day energy
- guests who want the cheapest room **and** the easiest non-bus park
  access — that is usually [pop-century-resort.md](pop-century-resort.md)
