# Be Our Guest Restaurant

Magic Kingdom Fantasyland signature table-service dining, themed to
the Beast's castle from *Beauty and the Beast*. Guests dine inside a
recreation of the ballroom and other castle rooms, with stone walls
and stained-glass windows.

## Menu and pricing

Prefix menu for all meals (lunch and dinner both served):

- **Lunch**: $72 per adult, $43 per kid, prefix
- **Dinner**: $72 per adult, $43 per kid, prefix

French-inspired cuisine. Example dishes: duroc pork chops, poulet
rouge chicken, filet mignon.

Historical note: Be Our Guest used to offer a quick-service lunch
option alongside the table-service dinner, but the quick-service
option has been eliminated. All meals are now expensive prefix.

## DFBGuide's verdict

**"You're paying for atmosphere, not food."**

DFBGuide's direct framing: the cuisine is "fine, like high-end
wedding food, but very clearly mass-produced. Getting better, but
still kind of what you're getting." The real product is the
experience of dining inside the Beast's castle.

Because the food is overpriced for its quality, **reservations are
easier to secure here than they used to be** — ADR availability has
improved specifically because price has pushed away some guests.
If the castle dining experience is a priority but you can't get
Cinderella's Royal Table, Be Our Guest is a fallback.

## Character experience

Be Our Guest is **not a full character meal** like
[Crystal Palace](crystal-palace.md). There is one character element:
**the Beast walks through the various dining rooms at some point
during each seating**. He does *not* stop for meet-and-greets or
photos — he passes through. Guests who want to actually interact
with characters while eating should book Crystal Palace or
[Cinderella's Royal Table](cinderellas-royal-table.md) instead.

## Table-location warning

DFBGuide 2026-03 adds a useful room-specific caution: many guests want
seating in the **West Wing** because it is the most atmospheric room, but
it is not automatically the best fit for every party.

The recurring thunder / lightning effects there can be a bad match for
sensor-sensitive kids or guests who wanted the castle vibe without the
storm-room intensity.

If room placement matters to your party, ask at check-in and be prepared
for the answer to be yes, no, or yes-but-with-a-wait.

## Worth it for

- Guests who want the castle-dining experience and missed on
  Cinderella's Royal Table
- Beauty and the Beast fans
- Parties specifically attracted to the architecture and room
  theming

**Not worth it for**: food-first diners, budget-sensitive trips,
parties expecting character interaction.
