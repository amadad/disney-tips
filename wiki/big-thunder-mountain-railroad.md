# Big Thunder Mountain Railroad

Magic Kingdom Frontierland classic mine-train coaster. Closed throughout
2025 for a major refurbishment and **reopening May 3, 2026** (announced
live during TheTimTracker's 2026-04-08 park visit, firming up DFBGuide
2025-12's "sometime in 2026, no date" and AllEars 2026-04's "about to
reopen"). First attraction in the wiki to go through a full three-source
supersession chain resulting in a firm date.

## Reopening

- **Official reopening**: **May 3, 2026**
- Cast-member previews and soft openings likely in the final days
  before that date
- **Height requirement lowered** from 40" to 38" — a meaningful change
  for families with young kids. TheTimTracker speculates the restraint
  design may have been updated, which would explain the relaxed
  threshold.
- **Trains actively testing** as of 2026-04-08 — TheTimTracker
  spotted trains running through scenes with no riders, suggesting
  the refurbishment work is essentially complete and cast training
  is underway

## Current state as of April 2026

Per TheTimTracker 2026-04-08 on-location observation: Big Thunder is
**quietly running** — trains visible moving through the track and
station. No rider audio (so the trains were clearly empty, meaning
these are operational and cast-training tests). Construction walls
still obscure direct views of the ride from guest areas, but side
paths in Frontierland offer partial sightlines.

The former Rivers of America area immediately adjacent to Big Thunder
is being filled in as part of the [Piston Peak National Park](magic-kingdom.md)
construction — the back-of-park landscape around Big Thunder is an
active construction zone.

## Trip planning

- **Trips before May 3, 2026**: Big Thunder is closed. Do not plan
  to ride it.
- **Trips May 3–15, 2026**: Expect high demand during the opening
  window. Even soft-opening and preview days will draw crowds.
  Lightning Lane pre-selection and rope-drop strategy both recommended.
- **Trips mid-May 2026 onward**: demand should normalize within a few
  weeks.

## Rope drop

See [strategy-rope-drop-magic-kingdom.md](strategy-rope-drop-magic-kingdom.md)
for the rope-drop path recommendations from AllEars. Summary: from
the hub, go through Liberty Square (not Adventureland — Adventureland
is a bottleneck). With Early Theme Park Entry, cut through the back
corner of Fantasyland near Memento Mori and Columbia Harbour House to
reach Frontierland more directly.

## Lightning Lane

Big Thunder Mountain Railroad is **expected to rejoin Tier 1** of the
[Lightning Lane Multi Pass](lightning-lane-multi-pass.md) system when
it reopens (per AllEars 2026-04 expectation, consistent with its
pre-closure tier). This means it will compete with Jungle Cruise,
Space Mountain, Peter Pan's Flight, and Tiana's Bayou Adventure for
your one Tier 1 pre-selection slot.

**AllEars's explicit recommendation during the reopening window**:
use Lightning Lane Multi Pass for Big Thunder; do not gamble on
standby. If the ride goes down on a given morning during the
shakedown period, standby will spiral fast, and your morning plan
will collapse.

## Open questions

- **New scenes during the refurbishment**: TheTimTracker hopes for
  the dynamite scene from the Disneyland version of Big Thunder.
  Disney has not publicly detailed which scenes changed or are new.
- **Station brake feel**: TheTimTracker notes the pre-closure
  version had a notoriously hard hit when reaching the station —
  curious whether the refurbishment softened it.
- **Full tier confirmation**: whether Big Thunder will actually be
  Tier 1 or Tier 2 on reopening is not yet confirmed by Disney.
