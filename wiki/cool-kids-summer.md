# Cool Kids Summer

Seasonal character-and-entertainment event running **2026-05-26 through
2026-09-08** across all four Walt Disney World parks. Returning (and
expanded) from its 2025 debut.

The branding is intentionally "for kids" but the event is built for all
ages — the name is a hook, not a demographic filter.

## What it is

Not a traditional festival with ticket add-ons or kiosks. Closer to a
season-long character takeover layered on top of normal park operations:

- Expanded character presence across parks, water parks, and resorts
- Spontaneous-feeling (but planned) character encounters
- Characters linger longer during interactions — less rushed than a
  standard meet-and-greet

## Resort hotel component

DFBGuide 2026-03 adds a hotel-layer detail that makes this event more
valuable for resort guests than the name alone suggests.

### Scheduled hotel character meets during the 2026 event

Disney says characters will appear at these hotels during the
**2026-05-26 to 2026-09-08** event window:

- **Art of Animation**
- **Caribbean Beach**
- **Pop Century**
- **Port Orleans Riverside**

The practical upside: families can get meaningful character interaction
**without park-ticket friction or giant in-park lines**.

### Pre-existing resort character pattern

DFBGuide also notes that resort character appearances had already begun
showing up at places like **Wilderness Lodge** and
**Port Orleans French Quarter** before this broader Cool Kids Summer
expansion.

## Returning from 2025

Per AllEars 2026-04, Disney is explicitly building on 2025's run. Confirmed
returning elements:

- **Goofy Core at Communicore Hall (EPCOT)** — indoor character-focused
  playground space. Described as "catnip for kids" in 2025 and nearly made
  year-round.
- Expanded character presence across all four parks, water parks, and
  Disney-owned resorts.

Other 2025 moments (not yet confirmed for 2026) that set the tone:

- **Clarabelle Cow at Hollywood Studios** — had her own picnic-table space
  with crayons and paper fans; would sit and color with guests with no
  rush-through.
- **Daytime dance parties in Magic Kingdom**.
- **Daisy at Restaurantosaurus (Animal Kingdom)** — now-closed venue;
  unclear where she will appear in 2026.

## Planning implications

Per the AllEars framing: Cool Kids Summer is not something to plan a day
around, but it is something **not to walk past**. Parties who treat every
unexpected character moment as a low-priority aside will miss the
concentrated payoff Disney is building into the summer.

DFBGuide 2026-03 adds two sharper planning uses:

- resort guests should pay attention to the **hotel character-meet
  schedule**, because those interactions may be easier than park-based
  ones
- **Early Theme Park Entry** during the event window may also include
  special character meet-and-greets, with different characters by park

That means Cool Kids Summer quietly increases the value of being a Disney
hotel guest during the event window.

Most relevant to:

- First-time visiting families with young kids
- Repeat visitors who have ridden everything and are looking for texture
- Parties with at least one adult Disney fan who values character
  interaction

Not relevant to: trips focused on thrill rides, dining, or crowd-avoidance
optimization.
