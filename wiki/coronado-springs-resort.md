# Coronado Springs Resort

Moderate resort that functions like **two different hotels in one**.
The important split is between the standard outer buildings and the much
more polished **Gran Destino Tower**.

## The split that matters

### Classic Coronado

The outer buildings are the older motel-style rooms around Lago Dorado.
These are still the regular moderate-resort lane.

### Gran Destino Tower

This is the hotel-within-the-hotel.
DFBGuide frames it as:

- sleeker
- more modern
- indoor-hallway hotel feel instead of motel feel
- steps from better dining and drinks

## Why people care about Gran Destino

It is the **only non-deluxe Disney hotel with club level rooms**.
That is a major distinction.

But DFBGuide adds an important warning:
**club level here does not unlock deluxe-resort park perks like
Extended Evening Hours.**

## Signature resort features

- **Lost City of Cibola** pool with huge pyramid centerpiece and jaguar
  slide
- broad activity lineup
- better food / drink density around the tower than most moderate resorts

## Best for

- guests who want a moderate price point with a more polished hotel feel
- travelers attracted to club level but unwilling to pay deluxe rates
- adults-first trips that care about bars / dining more than Disney-IP
  theming

## Not best for

- guests assuming Gran Destino club level equals full deluxe perks
- travelers who want heavy Disney character theming
