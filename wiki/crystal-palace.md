# Crystal Palace

Magic Kingdom table-service **buffet** character dining restaurant
located at the end of Main Street, just steps from Cinderella Castle.
Elegant Victorian architecture with glass-walled dining rooms.

## Format and characters

Buffet service for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. The character
experience is the **100 Acre Wood Winnie the Pooh crew**:

- **Winnie the Pooh**
- **Piglet**
- **Eeyore**
- **Tigger**

The conceit is that every day is **"Friendship Day"** — the
characters visit guests' tables throughout the meal, pose for
photos, and interact with kids. Unlike
[Be Our Guest](be-our-guest.md), this is a full character meal.

## Menu highlights

- **Lunch and dinner**: seasonal pastas, shrimp creole, country-fried
  chicken and gravy — DFBGuide describes it as "a very diverse
  spread of comfort food"
- **Breakfast**: standard morning buffet (eggs, bacon, sausage) plus
  Disney-specific standouts including **Mickey Churro Waffles**

## Reservations

DFBGuide's framing: **"probably the easiest to book on a regular
crowded day"** among Magic Kingdom's castle-adjacent signature
restaurants. It is still an advance dining reservation — 60-day
window — but significantly more accessible than
[Cinderella's Royal Table](cinderellas-royal-table.md).

## Worth it for

- Parties with young kids who want character interaction during a
  meal but didn't get Cinderella's Royal Table
- Guests who prefer buffet format over prefix (control over what
  and how much to eat)
- Winnie the Pooh fans specifically

**Not worth it for**: parties prioritizing princess interactions
(go to Cinderella's Royal Table instead), gourmet diners, budget
trips.
