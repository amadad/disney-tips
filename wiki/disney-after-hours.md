# Disney After Hours (Magic Kingdom)

Ticketed premium after-hours event at Magic Kingdom that clears the
park of regular-admission guests and allows ticket-holders access to
20+ attractions with minimal waits, included snacks and drinks, and
an exclusive fireworks show.

## 2026 dates

Disney After Hours at Magic Kingdom runs on **select nights from
January 12, 2026 through July 27, 2026**. Specific event dates within
that window vary — check the Disney World calendar or DFB's calendar
for the nights Disney After Hours is actually offered.

(Per DFBGuide 2025-12.)

## What you get

- **Access to 20+ Magic Kingdom attractions** including
  [Tron Lightcycle Run](tron-lightcycle-run.md) and
  [Seven Dwarfs Mine Train](seven-dwarfs-mine-train.md) — typically
  the park's longest waits, now running with **dramatically reduced
  waits** because general admission crowds are out
- **Included snacks and drinks** — ice cream, popcorn, and drinks are
  complimentary during the event, grab as many as you want with no
  need to budget
- **Exclusive Disney Enchantment fireworks** — a different fireworks
  show from the regular [Happily Ever After](happily-ever-after.md),
  performed only on Disney After Hours event nights
- **Character meet-and-greets during the event** — Mickey Mouse at
  Town Square Theater and princesses at Princess Fairytale Hall
  remain open and bookable with short waits

## Times

- **Ticket holders may enter the park as early as 7 PM** — this
  matters because regular guests without an After Hours ticket are
  still in the park until official close
- **Event officially starts at 10 PM** — this is when the reduced-
  crowd experience kicks in
- **Event ends at 1 AM** — three-hour formal event window
- **Both fireworks**: ticket holders who enter at 7 PM get to see
  **both** the regular Happily Ever After (at its scheduled time)
  **and** the exclusive Disney Enchantment at 10 PM — doubling up
  on fireworks is a real and intended benefit

## Pricing

Per DFBGuide 2025-12: prices can be "as expensive, if not more
expensive, than a regular day ticket." Capacity is capped, which is
what allows the low-wait experience. Specific 2026 pricing is not
captured in ingested sources — check the Disney World website for
your target date.

## Worth it for

- **Guests who want to marathon-ride headliners without waits** —
  especially Tron and Seven Dwarfs Mine Train, the two Single Pass
  rides. Disney After Hours is the one way to ride both repeatedly
  in one night.
- **Guests visiting Magic Kingdom for only one day** who cannot
  spend a full daytime session waiting in lines — the After Hours
  event compresses what would be a multi-day ride list into 3 hours
- **Disney regulars** who have already done the daytime MK
  experience and want something different
- **Summer trips** — DFBGuide notes: "the sun isn't trying to kill
  you," which matters for Florida summer guests

**Not worth it for**: parties with young kids on standard bedtimes
(event ends at 1 AM), guests on tight budgets, parties that want
parade and daytime character experiences (those don't happen during
the event window).

## Related

Disney After Hours is distinct from **Extended Evening Hours**, which
is a separate perk available only to deluxe-level Disney Resort
Collection hotel guests. Extended Evening Hours gives deluxe hotel
guests 2 additional park hours after the park closes to general
admission — but it does **not** include the free snacks, the
exclusive fireworks, or the full 20+ attraction list of Disney After
Hours.
