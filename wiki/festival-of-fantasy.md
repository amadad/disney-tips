# Festival of Fantasy Parade

Magic Kingdom's daytime parade — the flagship daily parade that
features large-scale floats, live costumed characters, music, and
Disney film tie-ins. Runs **twice daily** on most days.

## What to watch for

DFBGuide's highlight: **the fire-breathing Maleficent Dragon float**.
It is widely considered the parade's show-stopper moment. The dragon
appears roughly **two-thirds of the way through the parade**, so
from the start of a given viewing area's coverage, expect her around
the **8–10 minute mark**.

If you are choosing between watching the whole parade or timing your
viewing to catch the dragon specifically, DFBGuide's framing:
"Flynn Rider got away with that smolder, but the Maleficent dragon
tends to be the highlight."

## Showtime variability

Festival of Fantasy typically runs twice daily, but specific showtimes
vary by day of week and season. The **typical afternoon showtime is
around 3 PM** (per DisneyInDetail 2026-04). **DFBGuide's guidance**:
always check **My Disney Experience** at the start of your park day
to confirm the actual showtimes for that specific day — times shift
and occasionally the parade is cut to one showing.

## Related

Magic Kingdom runs a handful of smaller parade-style moments in
addition to Festival of Fantasy:

- **Disney Adventure Friends Cavalcade** — smaller sample-sized
  parade featuring characters including Nick and Judy (Zootopia),
  Baloo, Miguel (Coco), Merida, Moana, Elena, Toy Story characters,
  and Clarabelle Cow
- **Disney Starlight: Dream the Night Away** — separate nightly
  parade launched 2025, runs twice per night once before and once
  after [Happily Ever After](happily-ever-after.md), traveling a
  reverse route of Festival of Fantasy from Main Street toward
  Frontierland. Typical showtimes are around 8 PM and 10 PM (or
  sometimes 9 PM and 11 PM) per DisneyInDetail 2026-04; in quieter
  colder months the second showing may be cut and the parade runs
  only once per night. Music is specifically nostalgic for
  long-time guests because it references earlier nighttime parades
  that used to run at Magic Kingdom years ago.
