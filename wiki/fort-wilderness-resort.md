# Fort Wilderness Resort

Disney resort built around cabins, campsites, and outdoor recreation.
One of the most unusual lodging choices at Walt Disney World because it
is less like a conventional hotel and more like a Disney campground /
outdoor village.

## Why it stands out

Fort Wilderness is all about **options**.
DFBGuide highlights three major lanes:

- Disney Vacation Club **cabins**
- **campground** stays with your own camper or tent
- a large menu of outdoor recreation even on non-park days

## Cabins vs campground

### Cabins

The key selling point from this source:
DFBGuide says the cabins can sometimes cost about the same as an
Art of Animation family suite **while still giving you deluxe-resort
perks**.

### Campground

Cheaper, more flexible, but DFBGuide explicitly notes that campground
stays **do not get deluxe-resort perks**.

## 2026 changes to know

- campsite occupancy drops from **10 guests to 8 guests** starting in
  2026
- the **Meadow Swimming Pool** area is being upgraded, with the future
  plan including a new zero-entry pool area, larger deck, and pickleball
  courts
- the pool is **not closed** during this work, but seating is more
  limited and construction may be visible

## Transportation and scale

Fort Wilderness does have:

- buses around property / to parks
- boats to Magic Kingdom

But DFBGuide's warning is strong:

- travel times are a pain
- the resort is huge
- the **internal bus loop is confusing**
- renting a **golf cart** can meaningfully improve the stay

## Outdoor-activity signal

DFBGuide uses this as one of the resort's biggest advantages:

- Segway tours
- archery
- gem mining
- Chip and Dale campfire singalong
- pony rides
- fishing
- kayaking / canoeing
- Hoop-Dee-Doo Musical Revue

## Best for

- repeat visitors who want a different kind of Disney stay
- families who will actually use the outdoor activities
- groups that need cabin space and like the deluxe-perks angle
