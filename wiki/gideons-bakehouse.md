# Gideon's Bakehouse

Small gothic-style bakery in Disney Springs known for giant loaded cookies,
large cake slices, cold brew drinks, and very long lines. One of the clearest
examples in Disney Springs of a place where **timing matters as much as the
food itself**.

## Why it stands out

Gideon's is both:

- a genuine snack destination
- and a line-management problem

DFBGuide explicitly uses Gideon's as an example of why weekday-morning Disney
Springs visits can be strategically better than weekend / evening visits.

## Best time strategy

If Gideon's is important to your Disney Springs plan, DFBGuide's advice points
toward:

- **weekday** visits over weekend visits
- **morning** visits over late-afternoon / evening visits

The goal is to get ahead of the line rather than treating Gideon's as an easy
casual stop.

## Best-known items from this source

- fully loaded cookies
- specialty cold brews, especially peanut-butter-forward options
- giant cake slices large enough to stretch across multiple servings

## Worth it for

- snack-focused Disney Springs visitors
- bakery / dessert fans
- guests building a lower-cost Disney Springs grazing day

## Not worth it for

- guests who only want a quick no-line dessert stop at peak times
- people unwilling to schedule around crowd timing
