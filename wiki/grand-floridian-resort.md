# Grand Floridian Resort

Disney World's flagship luxury hotel.
Victorian styling, live music in the lobby, heavy restaurant density, and
one of the strongest Magic Kingdom-area locations on property.

## Why it stands out

Grand Floridian is the resort for guests who want Disney at its most
formal, polished, and expensive.

DFBGuide highlights:

- live pianist in the lobby
- Victorian theming throughout
- spa services
- major holiday displays
- walking-distance access to Magic Kingdom

## 2020s refresh cycle

DFBGuide emphasizes that Grand Floridian has changed a lot in recent
years, including:

- refreshed **1900 Park Fare** — now with Cinderella, Mirabel,
  Aladdin, and Tiana in the current character lineup per DFBGuide 2026-03
- refreshed **Narcoossee's**, **Victoria & Albert's**, and **Citricos**
- **Garden View Tea Room** reopening on **2026-03-19** with a refreshed
  Alice in Wonderland-flavored tea experience. DFBGuide 2026-01 treats
  Tea Room reservations as one of the harder ADRs to grab once they
  returned
- new lobby bar called **The Perch**
- refreshed **Mary Poppins-themed rooms**
- additional Disney Vacation Club inventory from the Big Pine Key
  conversion

## Dining signal

This is one of the strongest resort restaurant clusters anywhere on
property, including Disney World's only Michelin-starred restaurant,
**Victoria & Albert's**.

## Best for

- luxury-first trips
- guests who want easy Magic Kingdom access plus a high-end hotel
- travelers who care about fine dining and seasonal resort atmosphere

## Watch out

Even the most basic room categories here are expensive. This hotel only
makes sense if you actually value the flagship-resort experience.
