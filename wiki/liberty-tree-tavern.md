# Liberty Tree Tavern

Magic Kingdom Liberty Square table-service dining. **Family-style
all-you-can-eat American feast** — positioned as the "endless
Thanksgiving dinner" experience at Disney World.

## Format

Family-style service: one shared feast arrives at the table for
everyone to pass and share, all-you-can-eat. DFBGuide calls out the
dining room's signature detail — a framed portrait of a
"tomato baby" that appears to watch and judge diners from the wall.

## DFBGuide's context

Liberty Tree Tavern anchors what DFBGuide calls her **personal
favorite land for MK dining**. With the riverfront attractions now
permanently closed (Tom Sawyer Island, Liberty Square Riverboat, see
[magic-kingdom.md](magic-kingdom.md)), Liberty Square's attraction
count has dropped to just
[Haunted Mansion](haunted-mansion.md) — but the **restaurants in
Liberty Square have not been diminished at all**, and in DFBGuide's
view, they are the unsung heroes of Magic Kingdom dining:

- **Liberty Tree Tavern** (this page) — table service, family feast
- **Columbia Harbour House** — quick service, notable hush puppies
- **Sleepy Hollow** — quick service window, breakfast waffles
  (sweet and savory)

All three are in Liberty Square, all three are highly recommended by
DFBGuide, and none of them are famous the way Cinderella's Royal
Table or Be Our Guest are famous.

## Worth it for

- Large parties or families who want to share one meal
- Thanksgiving-style comfort food fans
- Budget-aware parties who want table service without signature
  pricing
- Guests who value Liberty Square's atmosphere and want to spend
  meal time in the quietest land of the park

**Not worth it for**: parties with small appetites (the value is in
the shared feast), picky eaters who want individual menu options,
parties wanting character interaction or princess experiences.
