# Mission: Space

EPCOT World Discovery simulator ride themed to astronaut training.
A genuine split-case attraction: the **Green Mission** is relatively
gentle and family-friendly, while the **Orange Mission** is one of the
most intense experiences anywhere in Walt Disney World.

## Green vs. Orange

- **Green Mission** — the easier version; family-friendlier and much more
  approachable for guests who just want the concept without the full-body
  punishment
- **Orange Mission** — the severe version; spinning, disorienting, and
  explicitly not a casual choice

DFBGuide's blunt advice: **if you get motion sickness of any kind, do not
ride Orange Mission**.

## Why Orange Mission gets such a warning

DFBGuide calls Orange Mission arguably **the most intense ride in Disney
World, period**. Her evidence is practical rather than theoretical:
Disney literally provides **sick bags on the ride** because guests get off
and immediately need them.

## Survival advice if you choose Orange anyway

If you insist on riding Orange Mission:

- keep your **head fully back**
- keep facing **straight forward**
- do not look around and try to fight the simulator
- do **not** stack it back-to-back with
  [Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind](guardians-of-the-galaxy-cosmic-rewind.md)
- consider **non-drowsy Dramamine** or motion-sickness patches before the
  trip if you already know you are prone to motion sickness
- DFBGuide's astronaut-training anecdote: **eat a banana first**

DFBGuide 2026-03's practical warning is that EPCOT's World Discovery area
can effectively become **"Barf Alley"** if you treat Mission: Space
Orange like just another filler ride.

If you forgot motion-sickness meds, Disney first-aid locations may have
sample-size over-the-counter options available.

This is the ride to treat with respect, not bravado.
