# Mobile Order

Disney World's in-app quick-service ordering system.

This is one of the easiest ways to save time in the parks, but it only
helps if you understand the one confusing part: **placing the order does
not automatically tell the restaurant to start making it**.

## Where it works

Per DFBGuide 2026-03 and current wiki coverage:

- Mobile Order works at **most Disney quick-service restaurants**
- some **third-party locations at Disney Springs** may not support it

## Basic flow

1. Open My Disney Experience.
2. Pick the quick-service location.
3. Choose a **pickup window**.
4. When you are actually close enough and ready, press the app's
   **"I'm here" / prepare my order** step.
5. Pick up the food from the mobile-order counter when it is ready.

## The part that confuses people

Mobile Order is **not** like some off-Disney fast-food apps where the
restaurant just starts making the food as soon as you pay.

Per DFBGuide 2026-03:

- the app usually prompts the final "I'm here" step about **5 minutes
  before** your pickup window begins
- if you never confirm that you are there, the order can appear to
  disappear when the window passes

That is the step first-timers miss.

## Why it matters

AllEars 2026-03 adds the most practical use case:

- if your group is getting hungry while you are still in a ride line,
  you can place the order **before** you reach the restaurant
- you can even place lunch orders **earlier in the day** if you already
  know where you want to eat

That turns a future lunch line into a short pickup stop.

## Recovery if something goes wrong

If your order seems to vanish because the pickup window passed:

- check your **order history** in My Disney Experience
- if needed, go to the restaurant's mobile-order pickup area and ask a
  Cast Member for help

DFBGuide 2026-03 frames this as recoverable in many cases, especially if
it has not been too long.

## Useful adjacent tricks

The source pair also reinforces three nearby quick-service rules:

- ask for **free cups of water** at quick-service locations instead of
  relying only on water fountains
- a **kids meal** can be the better reset move when the goal is to calm
  down, hydrate, and keep moving — not eat the biggest meal possible
- mobile ordering works best when it is helping solve a real park-day
  problem: heat, hunger, crankiness, or a tight schedule
