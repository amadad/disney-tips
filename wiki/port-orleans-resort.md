# Disney's Port Orleans Resort

Walt Disney World **moderate-tier** resort, New Orleans-themed, on the
Sassagoula River. Two sections under a single resort brand:
**Riverside** (the larger section, more activities) and **French
Quarter** (smaller, beignets, jazz club). **Pet-friendly** — one of
the Disney Resort Collection hotels where dogs can stay.

## Key facts

- **Tier**: Moderate
- **Price point**: ~$270/night as of 2026-03 (per PagingMrMorrow).
  Pricing varies by season and room category.
- **Location**: near [Disney Springs](disney-springs.md), on the
  Sassagoula River
- **Sections**: Riverside (with Alligator Bayou and Magnolia Bend
  sub-areas) and French Quarter
- **Shared-amenity resort pair**: yes — these two sections function more
  like sister resorts under one brand than isolated hotel wings
- **Pet-friendly**: yes (Disney Resort Collection policy applies)
- **Part of the [Disney Resort Collection](resort-perk-free-water-park.md)**
  — so guests get perks including free water park admission on
  check-in day

## Sections

### Riverside

The larger of the two sections. Two sub-areas:

- **Magnolia Bend** — southern mansion-style buildings with wrought
  iron details. More formal. Hosts wedding ceremonies outside the
  buildings. Includes Oak Manor building with the **recently updated
  rooms**.
- **Alligator Bayou** — more rustic bayou-themed buildings. Has not
  received the same room updates as Magnolia Bend's Oak Manor as of
  2026-03.

Riverside hosts most of the on-property dining and activities:

- Main Riverside pool with water slide and hot tub
- Muddy Rivers pool bar
- Fishing hole
- Horse-drawn carriage rides
- River Roost lounge (Yee-Haw Bob live performances select nights)
- Riverside Mill (quick service)
- Boatwright's (table-service)

### French Quarter

Smaller, more compact section about a 5-minute walk from Riverside (or
reachable by the resort boat). Essentially functions as its own
mini-resort within the larger Port Orleans brand.

- **Famous for Mickey-shaped beignets** (Riverside does not serve
  them) — sold in sets of 3 or 6. Lines can get long during busy
  weeks like Easter and spring break.
- **Scat Cat's Club** — live jazz nightly
- Own quick-service dining
- Own pool area
- Own bus/boat transportation connections

DFBGuide 2026-04 reinforces that Riverside and French Quarter are one of
the rare Disney resort pairs where guests can do a little **pool hopping**
between the two sections.

## Rooms

**Recently updated rooms** (Oak Manor building in Magnolia Bend, as of
2026-03) are themed to **Princess and the Frog / Tiana**. Features:

- Princess and the Frog artwork throughout
- Bayou-themed headboards
- "Tiana's Place" branded coffee corner (referencing
  [Tiana's Bayou Adventure](tianas-bayou-adventure.md))
- Jazz music soundtrack on the in-room TV
- Two sinks in the bathroom, substantial storage
- Dalmatian plush on the bed (part of Disney's in-room housekeeping
  touches)

PagingMrMorrow compares the updated rooms favorably to **Grand
Floridian** (a deluxe-tier resort), framing them as visibly more
polished than what Port Orleans offered before the refresh.

**Royal princess rooms** — a separate room type still available in
Riverside featuring multiple princesses (not the new Tiana theme).
Not updated in the same refresh. Confirm room category when booking
if the Tiana theme matters.

DFBGuide 2026-04 also notes that Riverside includes **fifth-sleeper
room** options beyond the standard four-person layouts.

Alligator Bayou rooms are **not visibly updated** in the same way as
Oak Manor per PagingMrMorrow's on-location read.

## 2026–2027 construction watchout

DFBGuide 2026-03 reports that **Riverside guest-room construction** will
continue through **August 2027**.

The source specifically frames **Magnolia Bend** as the section more in
construction-trench mode right now, while more of **Alligator Bayou** has
already moved through its update cycle.

## Amenities and activities

A relatively high amount of on-property activity for a moderate
resort — this is a point of pride for Port Orleans and a meaningful
factor in the "worth it for" framing below.

- **Fishing hole** (Riverside) — $7 per half-hour per rod. Old-school
  cane-pole style (no reel, no casting — drop the bobber off the
  dock). Hot dog bait provided; worms also available. Bluegill,
  largemouth bass, catfish, sunfish. **Catch and release** only.
  Last rod goes out at **1 PM**, so plan accordingly.
- **Horse-drawn carriage rides** — reservations by phone, frequently
  sold out. Walk-ups possible if there's a cancellation.
- **Main pool (Riverside)** — water slide and hot tub
- **Quiet pools** — one per building cluster, no slides, for guests
  who want a low-key swim
- **Campfire on the bayou** — evening activity, **free marshmallows**,
  s'mores kits for purchase
- **Muddy Rivers pool bar** — bar with games adjacent to the main
  pool
- **River Roost lounge** — bar with live music (Yee-Haw Bob
  performances select nights — a Port Orleans tradition)
- **Scat Cat's Club (French Quarter)** — live jazz, beignets on
  site
- **Arcade** in the main lobby, includes massage chairs
- **Weekly activity list** (rotates): mosaic class, movie under the
  stars, nighttime trivia, "Glow with the Flow" pool light show,
  Mickey tie-dye

## Dining

### Riverside

- **Boatwright's** — table-service. The featured offering is the
  **"Taste of the Bayou"** family-style all-you-care-to-enjoy
  skillet service: ribs, brisket, chicken, mac and cheese,
  bacon-sautéed corn, bread. **$38 per person**. Shrimp is a **$10
  add-on** (6 pieces, with chimichurri). À la carte entrées also
  available (roasted prime rib, jambalaya, shrimp pappardelle).
  **Peanut butter pie dessert is specifically flagged** by
  PagingMrMorrow as "actually phenomenal — best peanut butter pie
  I've ever had." The current Taste of the Bayou menu is barbecue-
  focused and represents an upgrade from the earlier fried-chicken
  version. Restaurant has a large central boat as decor (hence the
  name).
- **Riverside Mill** — quick service. **Actual New Orleans cuisine**,
  not generic theme-park food: jambalaya (PagingMrMorrow's usual
  order), gumbo, po'boys, beef po'boy. New items on the 2026 menu
  include lemon-pepper parmesan chicken strips. Mobile ordering
  supported. Prices around $12–$13 for entrée portions.
- **Muddy Rivers** — pool bar
- **River Roost** — bar/lounge food

### French Quarter

- **Mickey-shaped beignets** — the signature item. Sold in sets of 3
  or 6. Powdered sugar; ask for extra if you want it. Not available
  at Riverside.
- Quick service and additional bar food in the French Quarter lobby
  area

## Transportation

- **Bus** to all four Walt Disney World parks
- **Boat to [Disney Springs](disney-springs.md)** — ~10-minute ride,
  stops along the way. Nicer alternative to the bus for Disney Springs
  trips, and functionally a small amenity in its own right.
- **Walking or boat between Riverside and French Quarter** — 5-minute
  walk

## Check-in timing tip

Official check-in is 3 PM, but **in practice Port Orleans lets guests
check in as early as 11 AM** if the room is ready (per PagingMrMorrow).
This is a meaningful bonus for trip planning — if you arrive at the
resort mid-morning, drop bags and ask for early check-in rather than
waiting for the official time. PagingMrMorrow calls this out as a
"whole extra day" of resort enjoyment.

## Worth it for

- **Parties that want a moderate-tier resort with deluxe-feeling
  refreshed rooms** (Oak Manor / Tiana rooms specifically)
- **Pet owners** — one of Disney's pet-friendly hotels
- **Guests who value on-property activities** — fishing, carriage
  rides, campfire, pools, live entertainment at River Roost and Scat
  Cat's
- **New Orleans cuisine enthusiasts** — Riverside Mill quick service
  is unusually good for a Disney resort (jambalaya, po'boys, gumbo
  are the real thing, not theme-park versions)
- **Disney Springs-frequent guests** — the boat transportation to
  [Disney Springs](disney-springs.md) is the best-feeling transit
  option at any moderate resort
- **Guests who want a calm "resort day" experience** — PagingMrMorrow
  explicitly chose Port Orleans for a low-key stay during a health
  recovery period, which is a genuine use case

## Not worth it for

- **Guests prioritizing park transportation speed** — moderate-tier
  buses to parks; deluxe resorts with monorail or Skyliner
  connections get you into parks faster
- **Guests who want the absolute cheapest Disney resort** — value
  resorts (Pop Century, Art of Animation, All-Stars) are meaningfully
  cheaper
- **Parties wanting a themed-around-a-specific-franchise experience**
  — Port Orleans is New-Orleans-themed broadly, not Disney-IP-focused
  (despite the new Tiana rooms)
