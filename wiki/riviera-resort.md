# Riviera Resort

Elegant Disney Vacation Club resort on the Skyliner. The priciest hotel
in this transportation lane, but also one of the prettiest.

## Why it stands out

Riviera is for guests who want:

- Skyliner convenience
- a calmer, more polished resort atmosphere
- artistic theming with lighter Disney-character touches
- stronger room finishes than the value / moderate Skyliner hotels

DFBGuide frames it as the **most expensive Skyliner resort**.

## Signature identity

Theme is built around Walt Disney's European travels, so the resort leans:

- elegant
- airy
- artistic
- not too stuffy

The strongest visual callouts in the source are the **Peter Pan** and
**Tangled** mosaic passages near the Skyliner approach.

## Dining signal

Key standout here is **Topolino's Terrace**:

- breakfast character meal with Mickey, Minnie, Donald, and Daisy in
  artistic costumes
- upscale dinner at night
- EPCOT-fireworks views from its patio

DFBGuide also highlights quieter decompression spots like the lounge,
small cafe, bar by the pool, and the tiny beachside area.

## Room formats

Riviera offers:

- tower studios
- deluxe studios
- one-bedroom villas
- two-bedroom villas
- three-bedroom villas

DFBGuide specifically warns that some rooms, especially the smallest
**tower studios**, are tiny despite the high price.

## Best for

- Skyliner-loving adults or adult-heavy groups
- guests wanting a peaceful resort with upscale finishes
- travelers who care more about hotel mood than park-adjacent walkability
