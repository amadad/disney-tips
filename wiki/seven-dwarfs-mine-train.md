# Seven Dwarfs Mine Train

Magic Kingdom Fantasyland family coaster. One of the park's two most
demanded rides (the other being [Tron](tron-lightcycle-run.md)), with
wait times that often push past 2 hours by afternoon, especially in
the summer and on warm days. Family-friendly enough that parties with
young kids tend to prioritize it over Tron.

## Ride characteristics

Seven Dwarfs was **added to Magic Kingdom in 2014**. It is a
**family coaster with a 38-inch height requirement**, making it one
of the best starter coasters at WDW for young children who clear
that bar. Per DisneyInDetail 2026-04: "really smooth and quite
short" — a low-intensity coaster experience suitable for kids
working up to bigger thrill rides.

## Rope drop

Seven Dwarfs is **accessible during Early Theme Park Entry** — the
30-minute head start for Disney Resort Collection guests. It is one
of the two best rope-drop choices in Fantasyland, alongside
[Peter Pan's Flight](peter-pans-flight.md).

**Alternative timing**: DisneyInDetail 2026-04 notes that **right
at the end of the night** can also be a good time to ride Seven
Dwarfs, especially on busy days — the late-evening lull after
fireworks disperses the afternoon crowd.

DFBGuide positions Seven Dwarfs as the higher-demand of the two: "more
in demand" than Peter Pan, with the potential to push past 2-hour waits
by afternoon. Prioritize it first if you have early entry and your
party wants to ride it.

## Lightning Lane

Seven Dwarfs is one of two **Single Pass** attractions at Magic Kingdom
(the other is Tron) — it is *not* eligible for Multi Pass. See
[lightning-lane-multi-pass.md](lightning-lane-multi-pass.md) for
mechanics.

**DFBGuide's Single Pass choice**: if you can only afford one Single
Pass per day and your party skews toward younger kids or milder
thrill tolerance, pick Seven Dwarfs over Tron. For older or more
thrill-seeking parties, Tron has better return-time optics because
of its post-sunset lit canopy.
