## Brief

A high-yield source about **decisions that happen before a Disney trip is
ever booked**: which version of the year you are buying into, how dated
changes affect trip windows, where discounts meaningfully change the
math, which crowd spikes are easy to miss, how hotel perks should shape
hotel choice, how dining reservations really behave, and why even good
plans need backup routes.

This source matters because it pushes the wiki further upstream. It is
not just about how to use Disney once you arrive. It is about how to
choose the right trip in the first place.

14. Planning for Disney curveballs instead of assuming an ironclad plan

## Strategic pattern reinforced

**The most expensive Disney mistakes happen before the trip starts.**
This source reinforces that the product should help with booking-window
choices, not just in-park execution: date selection, hotel-perk fit,
pricing windows, reservation priorities, and contingency planning.
