## Brief

A high-signal source about the parts of Disney that confuse guests **before
anything even goes wrong**: what a ticket actually is, what the gate can
scan, when park reservations still apply, how hotel layers affect perks,
why 60-day booking feels stressful, how the app / website / phone split
actually works, and how many important reservations are not restaurants
at all.

This source matters because it turns several fuzzy planning ideas into
clear system rules.

## Strategic pattern reinforced

**A lot of Disney stress comes from category confusion.** Guests think
"ticket" means scannable entry, "on property" means Disney-owned,
"reservation" means restaurants only, and "Disney hotel" means all perks
are the same. This source helps the wiki answer those misunderstandings
more cleanly.
