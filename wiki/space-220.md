# Space 220

Highly immersive space-station restaurant at EPCOT.
One of the park's biggest atmosphere plays and one of its weakest pure
food-value propositions.

## The pitch

Space 220's selling point is not subtle: guests ride a space-elevator
"Stellavator" and dine **220 miles above EPCOT** with big space-view
windows. DFBGuide calls it EPCOT's most immersive restaurant.

## Prices and format

DFBGuide's February 2026 pricing snapshot:

- **Lunch** — prefix, 2 courses, **$55 per adult**
- **Dinner** — prefix, **$79 per adult**

Her verdict: the food is **just okay for the price**, which means most of
the money is going toward the environment, not the meal itself.

## Better way to do it

DFBGuide's recommendation is to aim for the **Space 220 Lounge** or a
same-day **walk-up bar seat** if possible. That gets you the room, the
Stellavator, and the atmosphere without forcing the full expensive
prefix-meal commitment.

## Worth it for

- guests who care deeply about themed environment
- one-time Disney splurge diners
- adults who want the lounge / bar version more than a full meal

**Less worth it for**: guests prioritizing food quality per dollar.
DFBGuide would rather spend serious EPCOT meal money at places like
Garden Grill or Le Cellier.
