# Tron Lightcycle / Run

Magic Kingdom Tomorrowland high-speed motorcycle-style coaster.
**Opened in 2023**. Widely considered **the most popular attraction
in the park**, consistently producing the longest waits —
particularly after sunset when the lit grid canopy is visible.

## Ride vehicle and accessibility

- **Motorcycle-style ride vehicle** — riders straddle a "lightcycle"
  and lean forward into the ride position. This is the signature
  visual feature but not everyone can comfortably sit this way.
- **Accessible seats are available** for guests who cannot ride in
  the motorcycle posture. Request at the entrance if needed.
- **No inversions** — Tron does not go upside down, despite its
  intense speed. Useful fact for guests who avoid inverting coasters.
- **Everything must be lockered** — guests cannot carry loose items
  on the ride because the speed will fling them off. Free lockers
  are available at the attraction entrance. Plan accordingly: remove
  phones, hats, sunglasses, keys, and anything in open pockets before
  entering the ride area.

## Lightning Lane

Tron is one of only **two Single Pass** attractions at Magic Kingdom
(the other is [Seven Dwarfs Mine Train](seven-dwarfs-mine-train.md)) —
it is *not* part of the Multi Pass tier system. Single Pass is à la
carte: one ride per purchase per guest.

**DFBGuide's recommendation if choosing one Single Pass at MK**: pick
Tron, unless your party skews very young. The reason is return-time
strategy — book a Lightning Lane return window for **after sunset**, so
you experience the ride with the lit grid canopy fully visible. Most
Disney rides peak in the morning; Tron peaks at night for this reason.

See [lightning-lane-multi-pass.md](lightning-lane-multi-pass.md) for
how Single Pass differs from Multi Pass.

## Early Theme Park Entry

Tron is a quirky case during Early Theme Park Entry (the 30-minute head
start for Disney Resort Collection guests). Guests are **allowed to get
in line for Tron during early entry**, but the ride itself does not
start running until official park open. The practical use: stake out
a low-wait position in the queue, store belongings in lockers during
that time, then ride the moment park officially opens.

Alternatively, Space Mountain is walk-on during early entry if you'd
rather save Tron for later in the day. DFBGuide frames this as a legit
choice between "use early entry to secure Tron line position" versus
"use early entry for a free Space Mountain ride."
