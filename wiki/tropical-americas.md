# Tropical Americas

Future Animal Kingdom land replacing the **former DinoLand U.S.A.**
footprint. **Not opening in 2026.** DFBGuide 2026-03 frames **later
2027** as the realistic opening window, which means 2026 guests should
think of this area as a construction project, not a near-term trip
feature.

## Current state

The final phase of DinoLand's closure wrapped on **2026-02-02**, after
which the area shifted fully into construction mode.

For 2026 trips, the practical result is simple: **this part of Animal
Kingdom is gone**.

That matters because DinoLand's removal is one of the reasons Animal
Kingdom now behaves like a **5-ride park** for rides-first itineraries.
It also removes former food locations and general park capacity, which
adds to the park's current under-construction feeling.

## What is coming

Per DFBGuide 2026-03, Tropical Americas will include major material from:

- **Encanto**
- **Indiana Jones**

The source transcribes the central village name as **"Pueblo
Espironza"**. Treat that village spelling as soft until a later source
firms it up; the stable high-level label here is **Tropical Americas**.

## Opening window

- **2026**: construction only
- **Later 2027**: expected opening window per DFBGuide's framing
- **2026 trips**: do not plan guest time here

## Trip-planning impact

For 2026 travelers, Tropical Americas matters mostly as an absence:

- fewer rides
- fewer dining / snack locations
- more visible construction energy
- stronger case for using Animal Kingdom either as a **half-day rides
  park** or as a **slower trails / shows / dining day**

If your party is visiting in 2026 because they are excited for Encanto
or Indiana Jones at Animal Kingdom, reset expectations now: this is a
**future-state project**, not current-state trip content.
