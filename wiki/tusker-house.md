# Tusker House

Animal Kingdom character buffet in **Harambe / Africa**.
One of the clearest all-around dining values in the park because it
combines a real meal, character greetings, and strong **Disney Dining
Plan** leverage.

## Why it stands out

Tusker House does several jobs at once:

- **character meal**
- **buffet**
- **one-table-service-credit value**
- **African- and American-inspired menu**

Characters called out by DFBGuide here: **Mickey, Donald, Daisy, and
Goofy**.

## Dining Plan value

DFBGuide's explicit call is that **Tusker House is the best use of a
standard table-service credit in Animal Kingdom**.

Why:

- **lunch and dinner** run about **$64 per adult** out of pocket
- those meals still cost only **one** table-service credit
- you also get the character meal and buffet format

That makes it a much stronger DDP play than:

- **Yak & Yeti** or **Rainforest Cafe**, which are à la carte
- **Tiffins**, which is signature dining and costs **two** table-service
  credits

## Cash-pay strategy

If you are paying out of pocket instead of using the dining plan,
**breakfast is the smarter splurge** because it is cheaper than lunch or
dinner while preserving the same broad value case: buffet, characters,
and a substantial sit-down break.

DFBGuide specifically calls out the breakfast spread as one of the best
on property, including **Simba waffles with banana caramel sauce**.

## Worth it for

- families who want one Animal Kingdom meal to do real work
- parties trying to maximize Disney Dining Plan value
- guests who want a character meal outside Magic Kingdom / EPCOT
- slower Animal Kingdom days built around rides **plus** dining and
  shows
