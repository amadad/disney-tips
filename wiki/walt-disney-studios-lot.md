# Walt Disney Studios Lot

New area at Hollywood Studios replacing the former **Animation
Courtyard**. Opens **later in 2026** (exact date TBD) and is a major
rework of a section of the park that had been underutilized for years
(MickeyViews 2026-04 frames the old Animation Courtyard as "the
Death Star theme and this blue color going on... concrete expanse").

The rebrand pulls Hollywood Studios back toward its original
"theme-park version of a real studio" concept, directly modeled on
Disney's working feature animation building in California.

## What's in it

### Magic of Disney Animation (updated attraction)

Replaces the Star Wars Launch Bay (which itself replaced the original
Walt Disney Feature Animation Florida demo, closed 2004). The rebuilt
attraction has multiple components:

- **Enchanted art gallery**
- **Drawn to Wonderland play area** — Alice in Wonderland-themed, for
  younger kids
- **Once Upon a Studio theater** — plays the Disney short film *Once
  Upon a Studio*, likely in the theater that previously hosted Star
  Wars trailers and the older Mulan animation demos
- **Animation Academy (returning)** — guests are guided through
  drawing Disney characters step by step

### Animation Academy's controversial instructor

Historically, Animation Academy was taught by a **live in-person
Disney animator**. In the 2026 version, this role is being taken over
by an **animatronic Olaf** (from *Frozen*). Pre-recorded guidance
clips from legendary Disney animators will chime in during the
experience.

MickeyViews flags this as **controversial** — guest reaction online
has been critical, framing it as Disney replacing live animators with
robotics. Disney's counter-move is the recorded-animator segments,
preserving some of the human instructional lineage.

**Characters Olaf will teach guests to draw**:

- Mickey Mouse, Minnie Mouse, Donald Duck
- Genie (Aladdin)
- Moana
- Stitch, Olaf himself
- Ursula (The Little Mermaid)
- Main characters from Zootopia

### Meet-and-greet roster

Inside the Walt Disney Studios lot area:

- Mulan
- Rapunzel
- Chip and Dale
- Donald and Daisy
- Goofy
- Stitch

### Disney Junior Mickey Mouse Clubhouse Live

A new interactive show replacing the former Disney Junior experience.
Note: this show **already exists** at Disney California Adventure; it
is being ported to Walt Disney World, not created new.

### New courtyard park area

The central plaza of the old Animation Courtyard — previously a
"concrete expanse" — is being reworked into a **planter-and-seating
area** with grass, trees, and room for families to park strollers
and sit down. MickeyViews frames this as "putting the *park* back in
*theme park*," a Jeff Vahle (WDW President) refrain.

## Exterior and theming

- **Facade**: inspired by the working Disney Feature Animation
  building in Burbank, California. Features a **sorcerer and Mickey
  hat** over the entrance and silver lettering in the California
  studio lot style.
- **New archway sign**: replaces the original Animation Courtyard arch
  (removed during construction; some guests including MickeyViews
  lament the loss of the original bas-relief). The new sign is modeled
  directly on the California studio lot entrance.
- **Color palette**: based on the colors used at Walt Disney's original
  Burbank animation lot, by his longtime designer.

## Trip planning

- **Trips before late 2026**: area is under construction, not yet open.
  The original archway is gone. Magic of Disney Animation is closed,
  Star Wars Launch Bay is gone, Disney Junior has cycled off.
- **Trips late 2026 onward**: a worthwhile area especially for families
  with young kids (Alice play area, Olaf Animation Academy, Mickey
  Mouse Clubhouse Live show, planter courtyard for rest breaks).

The space is **not headliner-replacement content** — it is a
supporting area with character meets, a kid-friendly attraction, a
show, and rest space. Trip planners should treat it as "a reason to
slow down in Hollywood Studios," not "a reason to add a day."
